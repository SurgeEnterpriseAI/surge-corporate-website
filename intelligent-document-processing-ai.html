Intelligent Document Processing with AI

Stop reading documents. Start acting on them — automatically.

Home › AI & Innovation › Intelligent Document Processing with AI

Intelligent document processing (IDP) uses AI to read, understand, and act on business documents — not just extract data from them. Modern IDP combines large language models (LLMs) and generative AI to summarise contracts, flag compliance gaps, and trigger downstream workflows without human intervention. The IDP market is projected to reach between $3.9 billion and $14.16 billion in 2026, growing at a compound annual rate in the high 20s to low 30s. Surge helps enterprises adopt and implement IDP on the Adobe Experience Cloud stack.

What intelligent document processing is — and why it matters now

Traditional document automation did one thing: pull data from a form and drop it in a spreadsheet. IDP in 2026 does something far more powerful. By combining generative AI and LLMs, modern IDP platforms can reason about a document's meaning — summarising a 50-page contract, checking whether liability clauses align with company policy, or cross-referencing an invoice against your ERP system and flagging discrepancies, all without a person reading a single line. Analysts call this shift 'cognitive extraction.' It moves IDP from a back-office processing tool to a genuine business intelligence engine. Another major leap is agentic AI: IDP systems that don't wait to be told what to do next, but autonomously take the logical next action the moment a document arrives — whether that's routing an insurance claim, escalating a compliance alert, or generating a summary for a sales rep. Real-time, event-driven processing has replaced old-fashioned batch jobs, so cycle times shrink from days to minutes.

  • Cognitive extraction: AI reads and reasons about unstructured content, not just structured fields — e.g. summarising a 50-page contract or spotting a non-standard clause.
  • Agentic AI workflows: the system autonomously decides and executes the next step — cross-referencing an ERP, flagging a discrepancy, or routing a record — without waiting for human input.
  • Real-time processing: documents are ingested and acted on the moment they arrive (triggered by an email receipt or form submission), cutting cycle times dramatically.
  • Industry-specific models: leading platforms are moving away from one-size-fits-all to solutions tuned for healthcare (patient records, claims), financial services (audit trails, regulatory reporting), and logistics.
  • No-code self-service: business teams — not just IT — can now design, test, and adapt document workflows using visual tools and pre-built templates.

Why the IDP opportunity is urgent for North American enterprises

North America is the largest IDP market in the world, accounting for more than a third (36.8%) of global revenue in 2025. The U.S. alone is projected to reach $5.07 billion by 2026, driven by digital transformation pressure in banking, financial services, insurance (BFSI), healthcare, and government — all sectors that manage enormous volumes of complex, regulated documents. Organisations that delay IDP adoption risk falling behind competitors who are already using agentic AI to process invoices, contracts, claims, and onboarding packs faster and with fewer errors. The window to build a competitive advantage through intelligent automation is open now — but it is closing.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is intelligent document processing (IDP) and how is it different from basic OCR or RPA?

Basic OCR reads text off a page. RPA moves that text between systems. IDP goes further: it uses large language models and generative AI to understand what a document means, reason about its content — flagging a non-standard contract clause or summarising a 50-page agreement — and autonomously trigger the next action in a workflow. It handles unstructured data that older tools simply cannot process reliably.

Which industries benefit most from intelligent document processing in 2026?

The biggest adopters are healthcare (patient records, claims processing), financial services (invoice automation, regulatory reporting, auditability), logistics, and government. The IDP market is increasingly moving toward industry-specific platforms that understand the unique compliance and data requirements of each sector, rather than generic, one-size-fits-all tools.

How big is the intelligent document processing market in 2026?

Market estimates vary by methodology: one analysis puts the global IDP market at $3.9 billion in 2026, while another projects $14.16 billion, with compound annual growth rates in the high 20s to low 30s. The U.S. market alone is projected to reach $5.07 billion. Whichever figure you use, the directional signal is the same — rapid, sustained growth.

What does 'agentic AI' mean in the context of document processing?

Agentic AI means the IDP system doesn't just process a document and stop — it autonomously decides and executes the next logical step. For example, after reading an invoice it might cross-reference your ERP, flag a price discrepancy, and route the exception to the right approver, all without human instruction. It shifts IDP from a passive processing tool to a proactive business intelligence engine.
