Multimodal AI for Digital Experiences

Turn text, voice, image, and video into intuitive, personalized customer experiences.

Home › AI & Innovation › Multimodal AI for Digital Experiences

Multimodal AI for digital experiences is AI that understands more than one type of input at once, such as text, images, voice, and video, to read user intent more accurately. In 2026 it has become a foundational part of personalized content, e-commerce product discovery, and content creation. As a Premier Adobe Experience Cloud Partner, Surge helps enterprises adopt, implement, integrate, and govern this technology so customers get more relevant, natural experiences.

What multimodal AI for digital experiences is and why it matters

Multimodal AI combines different data inputs, like text, images, voice, and video, to understand context and intent instead of relying on a single interface. The focus has shifted from the screen to the user's intent, with AI choosing the best way to respond. This is driving real growth: the global multimodal AI market is projected to reach $3.43 billion by the end of 2026, at a 37% annual growth rate. It now powers personalized content, intuitive shopping, and faster content creation.

  • Leading models in 2026, such as Google's Gemini 2.5 Pro, handle text, images, audio, and video within a single model.
  • In e-commerce, multimodal AI enables 'predictive product discovery,' where a shopper can describe a mood or intent and get curated product matches beyond simple keyword search.
  • An estimated 38% of business web content in 2026 involves AI assistance, up from 14% in 2024.
  • By the end of 2026, 40% of enterprise applications are projected to include task-specific AI agents, up from under 5% in 2025.

Multimodal AI and the Adobe shift to AI-first workflows

Adobe rebranded its Experience Cloud as 'CX Enterprise' in 2026, moving from tool-centric software to goal-oriented, AI-first, agent-based workflows, including new products like Adobe CX Enterprise Coworker designed to orchestrate complex marketing tasks. This aligns with the wider move toward AI agents that take action inside business processes, not just respond to text prompts.

  • AI determines the best interaction method, voice, chat, camera, or screen, based on context.
  • Agent-based architectures aim to orchestrate complex marketing and content tasks end to end.
