Conversational AI & Enterprise Chatbots

From scripted bots to agentic AI you can trust and control

Home › AI & Innovation › Conversational AI & Enterprise Chatbots

Conversational AI and enterprise chatbots are software systems that talk with customers and employees in plain language to answer questions and complete tasks. In 2026, they have grown from simple scripted bots into agentic AI that can run multi-step workflows. The global conversational AI market is projected to reach $20.7 billion in 2026. As power grows, enterprises now prioritize accuracy, transparency, and compliance over fully autonomous 'black box' tools.

What conversational AI is and why it matters

Conversational AI lets people interact with your systems using natural language instead of menus and forms. Today's enterprise chatbots are no longer simple scripts. They use large language models and Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)—a method that connects the chatbot to trusted internal data so it answers from real, current facts instead of guessing. This makes them act like integrated employees that can complete real work, not just chat.

  • The market is projected to reach $20.7 billion in 2026, up from $17.3 billion in 2025, and to exceed $27 billion by 2030.
  • Conversational AI in contact centers is projected to save $80 billion in labor costs by 2026 by automating routine support.
  • RAG is now the standard for enterprise chatbots because it connects them to trusted data sources and reduces hallucinations (made-up answers).
  • By 2027, 40% of new enterprise chatbots will be multimodal—able to handle text, voice, and images in one stream.
  • Enterprises are shifting from generic assistants to specialized agentic AI that executes workflows; for example, Klarna's AI handles 2.3 million conversations a month, equivalent to 700 full-time agents.

Why transparency and compliance now come first

As chatbots gain power, leaders worry less about capability and more about control. The top concern is 'black box' AI—systems that make decisions no one can explain or audit. Enterprises want conversational AI that is accurate, governed, and compliant, with clear visibility into how answers are produced.

  • 60% of enterprise leaders cite 'black box' issues and compliance as their top AI challenge.
  • 93% of leaders say AI transparency is critical for deployment.
  • Transparency has overtaken raw capability as the primary concern when choosing a solution.

What are conversational AI and enterprise chatbots?

They are software systems that interact with people in natural language to answer questions and complete tasks. In 2026 they have matured into agentic AI that can execute complex, multi-step workflows.

Why is RAG important for enterprise chatbots?

Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) is now the standard because it connects chatbots to external, trusted data sources like internal knowledge bases. This improves accuracy and reduces hallucinations instead of relying only on pre-trained models.
