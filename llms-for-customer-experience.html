What LLMs for customer experience are and why they matter

An LLM is an AI model trained on large amounts of text so it can read a question and write a useful answer. For customer experience, this means support is moving from agent-assist 'copilots' (which suggest replies to human agents) to fully autonomous AI agents that resolve repetitive, multi-step queries without human escalation. At the same time, consumers are changing how they shop: they now ask AI assistants detailed questions and compare options instead of using a traditional search engine. Brands that adapt can serve customers faster and meet them where they now look.

  • 87% of CX Trendsetters plan to use AI assistants across the entire customer journey by 2027 (Zendesk).
  • By the end of 2026, over one-third of UK shoppers will turn first to an AI assistant or LLM when shopping.
  • Nearly two-thirds of Americans now use generative AI as their primary online shopping assistant.
  • 'Vertical LLMs' are trained on industry-specific data to deliver compliant, context-aware interactions.
  • The AI in customer experience market is projected to exceed $117 billion by 2030.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are LLMs for customer experience?

They are large language models—AI trained on huge amounts of text—used to understand and answer customer questions in plain language. In 2026 they power everything from agent-assist copilots to autonomous AI agents that resolve complex requests end-to-end.

How do Vertical LLMs improve customer experience?

Unlike general-purpose models, Vertical LLMs are trained on specialized, industry-specific datasets. They understand industry jargon and integrate with internal customer records and external regulations, helping deliver tailored, compliant, and context-aware interactions.

Why are LLMs important for online shopping and discovery?

Consumers increasingly use AI assistants instead of search engines. Nearly two-thirds of Americans now use generative AI as their primary shopping assistant, and Adobe data shows retail web traffic from LLMs grew 393% between 2024 and 2025.
